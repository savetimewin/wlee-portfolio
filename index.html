Zap

Description:
Zap is minimalist survival shooter built with Python, using the Pygame library. You pilot a neon pink circle in an 800×600 arena, dodge procedurally spawned squares that constantly come toward the screen center, and fire 8‑way lasers with WASD/ZQECX inputs to clear space. The game tracks how long you last, ramps spawn rate and speed as minutes pass, and offers a simple start screen plus a “Try Again?” game-over loop so you can chase longer survivals.

Implementation:

  • Implemented the core game loop and state flow (start screen → gameplay → game over → retry).
  • Built procedural enemy spawning with simple “home toward center” movement and difficulty ramp over time.
  • Added player movement, 8‑way shooting, projectile/enemy updates, and collision detection for hits + death.
  • Created basic UI elements (start button, game-over prompt) and an on-screen survival timer.

Future implementation considered for the improvement of the game:

  • Add a point system where taking fewer steps to dodge awards points or less penalty — rewards efficient movement and encourages planning instead of constant strafing.
  • Add a center boundary area that grants more points — nudges players toward higher-risk positioning that forces sharper dodges.
  • Slow down the spawn rate and speed of squares and have them bounce back when hitting the edge — smooths difficulty spikes and keeps pressure without edge-stacking.
  • Add a leaderboard to see who had most points and longest survival time, and add your own records — gives replayability and a measurable goal for skill progression.

Magnet Bomber

Description:
Magnet Bomber is a top-down arena prototype where you weave through waves of enemies, plant bombs, and use magnetic pulls to drag threats into clustered explosions. Movement is twin-stick-like (mouse-directed motion plus A/D rotation), with a visible aim line and chamber indicator to signal bomb readiness.

Implementation:

  • Player controller handles mouse-directed movement, A/D rotation, cursor lock/hide, boundary clamping, a thin aim line plus chamber indicator, and bomb controls (Pull Bomb: S or Right Mouse Click; Push Bomb: W or Left Mouse Click).
  • Bombs support launch speed, acceleration, bounce force, timed lifetime, and explosive radius/duration, with a magnetic pull state to yank bombs or enemies into detonation zones.
  • Bombs use line-rendered indicators and layer-masked raycasts for targeting and collision handling.
  • Health and bounds systems keep the player survivable while reinforcing arena limits.

Future implementation considered for the improvement of the game:

  • Make bomb-pulls worth the risk by rewarding pre-detonation pulls (bonus damage, score multipliers, or cooldown refunds).
  • Design encounters where pulling beats dodging alone (shielded or clustered foes best cleared by magnetizing bombs).
  • Clarify high-value pulls with stronger visual/audio telegraphs so players see when risking a pull pays off.
  • Add pressure that pure dodging can’t solve (timed objectives or advancing hazards) so pulling becomes necessary.
  • Polish pull/launch feel—crisper beam feedback, tuned pull speed, and snappy launches to make the mechanic feel powerful.
